## Executive Summary

Nexus Hotel seeks to implement an innovative digital tool that replaces the traditional phone-based reservation process. The solution will enable existing and new customers to make reservations in a **fast, secure, and reliable** manner, guaranteeing the protection of their personal and banking information.

## Problem Statement

The current reservation method via reception presents significant challenges that impact both customer experience and business efficiency.

### Current System Limitations

<Warning>
  The traditional phone-based reservation system creates multiple vulnerabilities and inefficiencies.
</Warning>

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Inefficient" icon="clock">
    Manual processes lead to errors, delays, and inconsistent service quality
  </Card>
  <Card title="Slow" icon="hourglass">
    Limited by staff availability and working hours, creating bottlenecks during peak times
  </Card>
  <Card title="Vulnerable" icon="shield-exclamation">
    Exposes personal, financial, and preference data to cybersecurity risks
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Data Security Concerns

The system handles highly sensitive information:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Personal Information">
    Customer names, contact details, and identification documents
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accommodation Preferences">
    Room preferences, special requests, and stay history
  </Step>
  <Step title="Banking Data">
    Credit card information, payment methods, and transaction history
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  From the perspective of business executives, politicians, and everyday customers, a critical question emerges: **Is it safe for anyone with basic IT knowledge to access personal data and reservation details?**
</Note>

### Customer Trust Issues

Customers planning special events need confidence when sharing **banking data**. They must trust that the hotel implements both minimum and advanced **cybersecurity measures**.

The lack of modern security infrastructure creates:
- Hesitation to share payment information
- Loss of high-profile clients concerned about privacy
- Competitive disadvantage against hotels with secure digital platforms
- Reputational risk in case of data breaches

## Strategic Requirements

For project success, three critical requirements must be guaranteed:

### 1. High Levels of Security

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Technical Security" icon="code">
    - End-to-end encryption (TLS/SSL)
    - Secure authentication (JWT)
    - Row-level security (RLS)
    - Automated vulnerability scanning
    - Regular security audits
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Compliance" icon="clipboard-check">
    - GDPR compliance for EU customers
    - PCI DSS for payment processing
    - SOC 2 Type II certification path
    - Data privacy regulations adherence
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### 2. High Service Availability

<Warning>
  Any downtime directly impacts revenue and customer satisfaction. The platform must maintain 99.9% uptime.
</Warning>

**Availability Measures:**

- **Multi-replica deployment**: 3+ instances running simultaneously
- **Load balancing**: Traffic distributed across healthy instances
- **Health checks**: Automated monitoring and alerting
- **Auto-scaling**: Automatic capacity adjustment based on demand
- **Disaster recovery**: Backup systems and failover mechanisms
- **Geographic redundancy**: Multi-region deployment capability

### 3. Reliability in Data Handling

<Steps>
  <Step title="Data Integrity">
    ACID-compliant database transactions ensure data consistency and prevent corruption
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Transaction Security">
    All payment transactions are processed through secure, PCI-compliant gateways
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Data Backup">
    Automated daily backups with point-in-time recovery capabilities
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Audit Trail">
    Complete logging of all data access and modifications for security and compliance
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Risk Management

### Identified Risks and Mitigation

<Warning>
  Proactive risk management is essential for project success and customer trust.
</Warning>

<Accordion title="Security Breach Risk">
  **Risk**: Unauthorized access to customer data
  
  **Mitigation**:
  - Multiple layers of security (encryption, authentication, authorization)
  - Automated security scanning in CI/CD pipeline
  - Regular penetration testing
  - Incident response plan
  - Cyber insurance coverage
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Service Downtime">
  **Risk**: Platform unavailability during critical booking periods
  
  **Mitigation**:
  - 99.9% uptime SLA through redundancy
  - Automated health checks and failover
  - Geographic redundancy
  - Real-time monitoring and alerting
  - Backup phone booking system for emergencies
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Technology Adoption">
  **Risk**: Customers resistant to new digital platform
  
  **Mitigation**:
  - Phased rollout with early adopters
  - Comprehensive user guides and tutorials
  - 24/7 customer support during transition
  - Maintain phone option as backup
  - Incentives for digital adoption
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Scalability Challenges">
  **Risk**: System cannot handle unexpected growth
  
  **Mitigation**:
  - Kubernetes auto-scaling
  - Load testing before major releases
  - Monitoring and capacity planning
  - Cloud infrastructure flexibility
  - Performance optimization ongoing
</Accordion>
